TargetSpace is a shared evaluation apparatus for target-specific forecasting under partial observation, instantiated first in personal intelligence. It is complementary to physical-reasoning, video-generation, embodied-control, and forecasting benchmarks — it measures whether a model can become a predictive model of a particular target, not just a model of generic scenes or events.

Why now?

Ambient AI is moving from recording to predicting

Ambient AI recorders, wearable memory devices, smart glasses, and personal context systems are moving AI from episodic question-answering toward longitudinal experience modeling. TargetSpace-Bench evaluates what future-relevant states become inferable from passive audio, visual, and activity traces — against population base rates (R1) and the target's own routine (R2), under calibration and permutation-specificity gates. Wearables are the most visible instance, not the boundary: the same benchmark problem arises wherever an AI system accumulates longitudinal traces about a target — assistants, enterprise copilots, tutors, care systems, robots, recommenders.

◇

Not just knowledge — experience

Documents reveal what was written. Ambient traces reveal what was lived, repeated, deferred, promised, pursued, seen, and acted upon. Knowledge is episodic and declarative; experience is longitudinal, situated, relational, embodied, multimodal — and predictive.

◈

From audio to audiovisual capture

Audio reveals speech, commitments, relationships, and topics. First-person video adds embodied context: objects, screens, faces, locations, documents, routines, and physical actions. In the evidence ladder these are simply tiers — whether richer capture pays is measured by the ablation, never assumed.

Why TargetSpace exists

A capability today's benchmarks under-measure

Deployed agents must reason about particular people, teams, systems, and projects over time — inferring latent goals, detecting behavioral shifts, updating beliefs, and forecasting target-specific futures. Most evaluations never test that.

What current benchmarks measure

  • Physical plausibility, object permanence, intuitive physics
  • Video realism and short-horizon scene continuation
  • Embodied control and manipulation success
  • Probabilistic forecasting of generic public events
  • Single-turn answer correctness and retrieval

What TargetSpace adds

  • Target-specific adaptation over a tracked instance
  • Longitudinal memory and belief updating from passive evidence
  • Goal / behavior transition detection
  • Multimodal evidence integration, measured by ablation
  • Counterfactual target forecasting, calibrated and sealed
◎

What it measures

Whether prediction of a target's future state improves when a model is given the correct target's history in the correct temporal order — over the population prior (R1) and the target's own routine (R2).

⛒

What it does not

It is not a video-realism, robotics, or intuitive-physics benchmark, and a high score implies nothing about those. It claims nothing about consciousness or a person's inner life.

⚖

How it's judged

Strictly proper scoring (log score in bits, Brier), gated by calibration and a permutation specificity test that must collapse skill on the wrong target.
