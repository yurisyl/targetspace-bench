Abstract

AI systems increasingly claim to know, remember, personalize to, and act on behalf of particular users, yet current evaluations cannot distinguish genuine person-specific predictive skill from retrieval, preference replay, imitation, or predicting what usually happens. TargetSpace turns that claim into a prospective sealed benchmark: a model receives observations of a target up to a sealed time and must emit calibrated probabilistic forecasts of the target's future target-state transitions before those outcomes exist. A stack of controls makes target-specificity the measured quantity — a population-prior baseline (R1), a person-specific own-routine baseline (R2), a calibration gate, a permutation specificity test under which forecasts scored against the wrong target must lose their skill, and an evidence-tier ablation — compared architecture-neutrally on identical sealed instances. The benchmark is motivated by an arriving capability class: AI systems that accumulate longitudinal observations about a target and convert them into future-relevant inference, made newly concrete by ambient recorders and first-person audiovisual devices, which motivate the benchmark but do not define its boundary. This is a pre-pilot protocol proposal: no empirical results are reported, and only the personal track is instantiated (synthetically).

Positioning and using TargetSpace

A summary of the paper's positioning section.

A complementary, under-measured axis

TargetSpace is not the "best world-model benchmark overall." It addresses a distinct, under-measured axis — target-conditioned longitudinal world modeling — that physical-plausibility, generation-fidelity, and control-success benchmarks do not, by construction, score.

Compared on shared axes only

Physical reasoning, video generation, embodied robotics, symbolic forecasting, and agent memory are complementary families. TargetSpace is compared with each only where they overlap — see the comparison table.

A battery of controls

Apply it as a protocol: zero/short/longitudinal history, shuffled-history and wrong-target controls, modality ablation, oracle and human anchors, calibration, and evidence attribution. See Baselines.

Positioning statement

TargetSpace evaluates a missing layer — whether a model can transform passive longitudinal observation into a target-specific predictive model — central for agents that reason about particular people, teams, systems, or environments over time.
